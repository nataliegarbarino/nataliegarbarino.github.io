Meet the
Cheetah

1992 World's Fastest Bike

In September 1992, the UC Berkeley alum-engineered Cheetah set a world speed cycling record of 68.73 mph for the 200 meter flying start event.

What is the Cheetah?

The Cheetah is a custom-built semi-recumbent bicycle created by three mechanical engineering graduates from UC Berkeley: Jon Garbarino, James Osborn, and Kevin Frantz.

Weighing 29.5 pounds, the bike features composite materials in its construction which includes an eight-pound aerodynamic fairing.

Evolution of the Cheetah

Explore the UC Berkeley Human Powered Vehicle (HPV) club prototypes that inspired the Cheetah.

1986

UCB three-Wheeler

About four years before the Cheetah this three-wheel bike was designed by the UCB HPV club for college competitions.

1987

Vitesse

Like its predecessor, the “Vitesse” also featured a three-wheel bike, and a low-sitting fairing.

1988

Concept Z

Concept Z was developed by the UC Berkeley Human Powered Vehicle Club, including the Cheetah engineers, for college-level competitions.

Summer 1988

Concept Z

This variation of Concept Z featured a refreshed paint job.

1989

Concept Z

This variation of Concept Z was virtually the same as its predecessor, only its fairing was reconstructed and repainted for further competition.

1990

Cheetah

Taking much of its inspiration from Concept Z, the Cheetah optimized this design for greater aerodynamics and speed.

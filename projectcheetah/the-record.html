Road to the record

68.73
MPH

On September 22, 1992, cyclist Chris Huber rode the Cheetah to a new world speed record. Recording an average speed of 68.73 mph through the speed trap, the Cheetah shattered the previous record of 65.48 mph.

In June 1990, the Cheetah was complete and the Cheetah Team headed out to the Bryce Canyon Airport (7600 ft elev.) for official attempts to break the world speed record (Gold Rush 65.48 mph, 1986).

After multiple test runs and one official attempt which concluded with a crash and slide down the runway at 59 mph, the collegiate record for human power was conquered (57 mph), but it was clear that the Cheetah needed more distance than the Bryce Airstrip could provide.

Changing plans on the fly, the team moved on to an airport in Gunnison, Colorado for more attempts and more challenges. The Gunnison Airport saw significant air traffic and scheduling runs under legal wild conditions proved difficult. The result was more failure, and the team returned home, found jobs, and it seemed that their dream had died.

Two years passed, and the question of the Cheetah’s unrealized potential still nagged at the three engineers, but Dexter Hysol had lost their belief. This time, the engineers would need to invest in themselves.

They initially traveled to a site in Alamosa County, Colorado where another team had made attempts many years before, but when they arrived they found the road to be in a state of disrepair and too rough to effectively challenge the record.

Fortunately, in the course of their travels in the area they found the six-mile lane just down the road from the Great Sand Dunes National Monument and in the shadow of the Sangre De Cristo Mountains. It was almost flat but more importantly it had the distance, and the greatest traffic came from local bison which roamed the landscape on either side of the road.

The team first eclipsed the record on the evening of September 21st, traveling over 66 mph. It seemed unreal after so much disappointment over so many previous efforts, but on the following evening at 6:52 PM on September 22nd, with a barely measurable breeze, Pro Cyclist Chris Huber powered the Cheetah to an unimaginable 6.510 seconds through the 200-meter speed trap and the new world record was 68.73 mph.

The cyclist

Chris Huber

Chris "The Punisher" Huber rode the Cheetah to a new world speed record for HPV's at an average speed of 68.73 mph over a 200 meter speed trap. Chris, a professional cyclist, rode and competed with the Coors Light Cycling team at the time of this historic ride.

The record

MPH

On September 22, 1992, cyclist Chris Huber rode the Cheetah to a new world speed record. Recording an average speed of 68.73 mph through the speed trap, the Cheetah shattered the previous record of 65.48 mph.

On September 22, 1992 in Colorado's San Luis Valley at approximately 6:55 PM mountain standard time, Cyclist Chris Huber rode the Cheetah to a new world record for the 200 meter flying start event. The Cheetah recorded an average speed of 68.73 mph through the speed trap, shattering the previous record of 65.48 mph.

The 200 meter flying start event allows a virtually unlimited run up to a 200 meter speed trap. The course was on Six Mile Lane, a flat road which crosses a Bison preserve in the San Luis Valley.

The cyclist

Chris Huber

Chris "The Punisher" Huber rode the Cheetah to a new world speed record for HPV's at an average speed of 68.73 mph over a 200 meter speed trap. Chris, a professional cyclist, rode and competed with the Coors Light Cycling team at the time of this historic ride.

The course

The course was on Six Mile Lane, which crosses a Bison preserve in the San Luis Valley. This long stretch of flat road was well suited for the 200 meter flying start event, which allows a virtually unlimited run up to a 200 meter speed trap.
